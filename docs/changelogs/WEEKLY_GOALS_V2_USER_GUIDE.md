## Tips for Success

### 1. **Start Small**
Don't overwhelm yourself. Start with:
- 1-2 activities per day
- Focus on weekdays first
- Add weekends later

### 2. **Be Realistic**
- Consider your schedule
- Morning person? More Monday activities
- Busy Wednesdays? Lighter goals
- Weekends for catching up

### 3. **Mix It Up**
- Don't do just reading every day
- Balance all skills:
  - Input: Reading, Listening
  - Output: Speaking, Writing
  - Practice: Conversation

### 4. **Use Weekends Wisely**
- Light maintenance (1 activity)
- Catch up on missed weekday goals
- Or rest completely!

### 5. **Multiple Languages**
If learning multiple languages:
- Dedicate days to specific languages
- OR mix activities each day
- Example:
  - Mon: Kannada focus
  - Tue: Hindi focus
  - Wed: Mixed day

### 6. **Track and Adjust**
- Check Weekly Overview regularly
- If you're not meeting goals: reduce them
- If goals are too easy: increase them
- Adjust based on real performance

### 7. **Celebrate Progress**
- Green checkmarks are achievements!
- Completed days deserve recognition
- Review past weeks to see improvement

## FAQ

**Q: Can I have different goals for different languages?**
A: Yes! Each day can have activities from multiple languages.

**Q: What if I miss a day?**
A: No problem! Your weekly plan stays the same. Just continue with tomorrow's goals.

**Q: Can I set goals for weekends now?**
A: Yes! Saturday and Sunday are now included.

**Q: How far ahead can I plan?**
A: You can view and plan up to 4 weeks in the future.

**Q: Can I see my past weeks?**
A: Yes! Use the Weekly Overview section and navigate back with â† arrow.

**Q: What if I complete more activities than planned?**
A: That's great! They'll all be tracked in the progress view.

**Q: Can I copy last week's goals to this week?**
A: Not yet - this is a planned feature!

**Q: Do I need to set goals for every day?**
A: No! You can leave days empty if you want rest days.
